## Executive Summary

The Shining Force fandom remains a passionate, dedicated community despite years of franchise dormancy. While smaller than Fire Emblem's fanbase, SF fans are highly engaged in modding, rom hacking, and community projects. The data reveals a hunger for modernization that respects the original spirit, particularly the unique blend of tactical combat with RPG exploration that distinguishes Shining Force from pure strategy titles.

**Key Finding**: There is a significant opportunity for a platform that enables fan creation without the limitations of ROM hacking, particularly one that preserves SF's defining characteristics while incorporating modern quality-of-life improvements.

## 1. Community Sentiment Analysis

### General Mood & Activity Level

**Status**: Active but frustrated with official franchise neglect

The community remains vibrant across multiple platforms:
- **Shining Force Central Forums**: The largest SF hub, showing consistent activity through 2025
- **r/ShiningForce subreddit**: Active discussions, with the Discord noted as "probably the most active currently"
- **SFMods.com**: Thriving modding community with 752 Discord members
- **Translation Projects**: SF3 Translation Project released V25 in July 2024 with quality-of-life improvements

Recent forum activity includes:
- "Chaos Randomizer" thread updated November 20, 2025
- Regular discussions about character balance, builds, and strategies
- Active Let's Plays and streaming of classic titles
- Ongoing mod development and releases

**Community Size**: Smaller than Fire Emblem but highly dedicated. Forum shows 21+ concurrent users regularly, with a peak of 3,004 users recorded October 6, 2025.

### What Fans Are Passionate About

The data reveals fans treasure these specific elements:

1. **Character Building & Army Development**
   - "Building up each character in the army is one reviewer's favorite element in the franchise"
   - The promotion system is sacred - fans specifically mention the satisfaction of seeing units transform
   - Large, diverse rosters (12 active + reserves) enable personalization
   - "One of the most fun aspects is how many optional or secret members you can pick up along the way"

2. **Character Diversity & Personality**
   - "Flying warrior birds, a kooky inventor/aviator, a steampunk-ish mech fighter, a floating jellyfish-type creature, a buglike assassin, and a baby dragon"
   - Each character having distinct personality, even if not plot-critical
   - "You'll agonize about which to take into battle" due to attachment to characters

3. **Exploration Between Battles**
   - "The ability to explore towns and the world map both set Shining Force apart from other tactical RPGs"
   - "The exploration mode that occurs outside of battle is essentially a Japanese-style traditional RPG game along the lines of Final Fantasy or Dragon Quest"
   - SF2's free-roaming Caravan system particularly beloved

4. **Accessibility & Simplicity**
   - "Part of the Shining Force charm is its simplicity. Anyone can pick up the game and begin playing it"
   - "The game is much more accessible than most RPGs"
   - "Very forgiving so experimentation is encouraged"
   - No permadeath pressure allows players to learn without constant restarts

5. **Battle System Design**
   - "Downright fun and addictive" tactical combat
   - Thoughtfully designed battlefields with strategic positioning
   - Top-down perspective (vs. isometric like FFT)
   - Freedom to choose tactics without feeling locked into specific strategies

### Common Complaints & Frustrations

The community consistently identifies these pain points:

1. **AI Issues (SF1/2)**
   - "The biggest issue is that the AI is bafflingly bad. Enemies will sometimes run away from you for no reason or skip a finishing blow in favor of attacking another party member"
   - "When enemies only have attack and spell as their options in combination with their AI that says 'make a straight line to the enemy' it's easy enough to guess how enemies behave"
   - Community mods addressing this get high praise: "The AI upgrade was welcome and it actually made me strategize a lot more"

2. **Balance Problems**
   - **Class Distribution**: "The game is 'knight heavy', giving the player 8 lance/spear users, when all other classes have only 2/3 members"
   - **Late Joiners**: "Some of the late-game characters (Chapters 6-8) are significantly weaker by the time they join and are left un-promoted"
   - **Egress Exploit**: "Max's Egress spell... can be repeated as many times as a player wants" to grind with no risk
   - **Level-Up RNG (SF1)**: Random stat gains vs. SF2's consistent progression

3. **Franchise Neglect by SEGA**
   - "The SF series deserves more respect than it's getting"
   - Mobile game announcement (Heroes of Light & Darkness) "looked like a gacha game situation" - met with mixed feelings
   - Fans note SEGA was "considering making a mobile game for Shining Force with impressive artwork" but it became vaporware
   - General sentiment: grateful for ANY new SF content, but wary of exploitative monetization

4. **GBA Remake Controversies**
   - Graphics/art style divisive: "The graphics to me and new art style are way worse, especially the character portraits"
   - New content criticized: "Teenage fanfiction-like plot rewrite and stupidly overpowered extra characters"
   - "Felt like a 12 year old remade it and put their fantasy characters in who are like, soooo awesome and totally invincible. And have trading cards, 'cos they're awesome!"
   - Balance issues: "Mawlock and Narsha exist, Arthur went from having a point to be trained to having the dumbest redeeming quality ever"

### What Fans Wish SEGA Would Do

1. **Shining Force 4 in Classic Style**
   - "Shining Force 4 needs to happen, and it needs to be in glorious, high bit pixel art"
   - Desire for a traditional tactical RPG, NOT action-RPG like Neo/EXA
   - Respect for the franchise's tactical roots

2. **Proper Remasters/Remakes**
   - "Remastering/remaking the classic Shining Force main storyline games" given "the rise (overwhelming success) of remasters and remakes"
   - With modern QoL but preserving original spirit
   - Some fans "abhor certain quality of life changes" - want options/toggles

3. **NOT Gacha/Mobile Exploitation**
   - Strong negative reaction to monetized nostalgia manipulation
   - "If the choice was a mobile gacha or nothing, I'd rather have nothing"
   - Disappointment with similar treatment of Final Fantasy Tactics, Langrisser mobile

4. **SF3 Trilogy Complete Release**
   - Fan translation project shows ongoing demand
   - Never officially released in full outside Japan

## 2. Rom Hacks & Fan Projects Survey

### Successful ROM Hacks

**Shining Force Alternate (SF1)**
- Status: "One of the best Shining projects ever" - "you NEED to play this" according to fans
- Features: Combines elements from all SF games, upgraded AI, new graphics
- Why Successful: "The AI upgrade was welcome and it actually made me strategize a lot more compared to the original, where some enemies would stay static the whole fight"
- Version: 3.2B (long development, still supported)

**SF2 Maeson**
- Focus: Enhanced enemy AI and challenge
- Features: "Enemies have been modified with tweaked AI - they can now use items to attack or heal, use spells much more often, including Status Effect Spells"
- Reception: Positive for adding genuine difficulty

**SF2 ULTRA**
- Focus: Graphics overhaul and balance changes
- Praise: "New character portraits are amazing... first hack that changed the portraits AND changed the speaking and blinking animations correctly"
- Features: New enemies, heroes, no duplicate monsters

**SF2 Battle Royale**
- Scope: "Perhaps the most complete game redesign of any hack"
- Changes: New characters, battle maps, music, items, monsters, graphics
- Unique Feature: Choose entire force from start, removes story segments
- Reception: Praised for scope and allowing immediate tactical experimentation

**SF2 Eternity**
- Status: "The first true game built in the disassembly"
- Focus: Content expansion - "more spells, more items, more classes, more battles, more shops, more everything"
- Balance: "The beginning is brutal in difficulty, but eases off after reaching Parmecia"

**REV 1 (SF1)**
- Approach: "Ultra minimalist collection of fixes and quality of life improvements"
- Philosophy: Community-curated bug fixes without major changes
- Reception: Appreciated as a "vanilla+" experience

### Why These Succeeded

Common success factors:
1. **Improved AI** - Consistently praised when done well
2. **Respectful to Original** - Even major overhauls maintain SF's core identity
3. **Balance Focus** - Addressing class imbalances, late-joiner problems
4. **Polish** - Proper portrait animations, attention to detail
5. **Clear Vision** - Whether minimal fixes (REV 1) or total conversion (Battle Royale)

### ROM Hack Limitations That Modders Face

Based on fan remake discussion and modding tools:
- "Limited color palette for sprites"
- "Limited control over animations"
- Resource limits in ROM space
- Difficulty adding truly new mechanics beyond stat/AI tweaks
- Can't fundamentally change UI/presentation

A fan remake project specifically cites wanting to be "modifiable and expandable" with "freedom to add and develop without being held back by rom modding limitations."

### Failed or Criticized Projects

**GBA Remake (Official, but instructive)**
- Why Criticized: Art style change, overpowered new characters, "fanfiction-like" story additions
- What Worked: Some QoL features, card system had niche appeal
- Lesson: Fans accept new content IF it doesn't overshadow or trivialize original experience

- This itself is instructive: fans remember successful hacks, forgotten ones leave no trace

### Modern Fan Game Attempts

## 3. Remake/Remaster Opinions

### GBA Remake Reception Summary

**Positive Aspects Fans Recognized:**
- "One of the best things about the remake is that nothing was tinkered with or changed just for the sake of it"
- Quality-of-life improvements: 4 item slots + 4 equipment slots (less inventory juggling)
- New music arrangements
- "Completely new graphics" appreciated by some
- Expanded storyline with Narsha subplot
- Card collection system - niche but some found it "fairly useful"
- Turn order visibility based on speed stats
- Ramping difficulty system (stars for replay, stat boosts)

**Critical Responses:**
- "The game just lost that magic somewhere; it didn't have the same feeling as the original thanks to the shiny, shiny graphics"
- Art style: "The graphics to me and new art style are way worse, especially the character portraits"
- New characters: "Stupidly overpowered extra characters... felt like a 12 year old remade it"
- "The Genesis/Megadrive version is way better than the Game Boy Advance version, only because it doesn't have the stupid extras thrown in"
- "Some of the original's fans think Resurrection of the Dark Dragon has a spirit problem"

**Balanced Take:**
- "A mostly successful remake that preserves the original's spirit despite some odd additions"
- "A fresh and interesting alternative to the original game that doesn't supersede it but is still worthwhile in its own right"

### Opinions on Potential Modern Remakes

**What Fans Would Accept:**
- Updated translations, improved interfaces, better graphics
- GBA QoL features incorporated (item/equipment slots, turn order display)
- Toggle options: "Let players choose their preferred experience - SF1 style displays, use SF2 styled displays, or have a more modern approach"
- "Bring in as much conveniences as you'd like or stay as true to the original Genesis version as possible, with user choice supported"
- Fixes to level-up RNG (match SF2's system)
- Better status overview during combat (table stats view from SF2/3)

**What Fans Would Reject:**
- Overpowered new characters that trivialize original content
- Forced art style changes without option to use original aesthetic
- Mobile gacha mechanics or exploitative monetization
- Removal of exploration/town segments
- Permadeath without it being optional
- Changes that make the game "easier" without toggle options

**Wishlist Features for Modern Version:**
- "Roguelike mode so you have an endless amount of randomly generated battles"
- "Explore Metapha more, make the game more sci-fi"
- Improved item system with better management
- More content, more battles, expanded story
- "Alternative promotions" - multiple paths for character development
- Enemy control chest codes for rare items
- "Open source code and documentation" for maximum moddability

### Purist vs. Modernizer Dynamics

**Purist Faction:**
- "Some fans actually 'abhor certain quality of life changes' in modern remasters"
- Value the original experience as-is
- Want bug fixes and translation improvements, minimal else
- Example: REV 1's minimal approach gets respect

**Modernizer Faction:**
- Want SF mechanics with modern conveniences
- Appreciate features like auto-search (SF2), better inventory
- Desire deeper tactical options, improved AI
- More permissive about new content if well-integrated

**Middle Ground (Majority Position):**
- "Sane defaults as a starting point" with options to customize
- Preserve core identity while fixing obvious problems (AI, balance)
- New content acceptable if OPTIONAL and doesn't overshadow original
- Graphics updates okay if art STYLE remains faithful

**Critical Insight**: This isn't a 50/50 split - most fans occupy the middle ground and want CHOICE. The controversy over GBA remake wasn't about the new features existing, but about them being forced and unbalanced.

## 4. Feature Wishlist Research

### Quality-of-Life Improvements Fans Want

**Inventory & Item Management:**
- Automatic inventory distribution
- Centralized storage system (Caravan improvement)
- 4 item + 4 equipment slots (from GBA)
- Better interface for item transfers between characters

**Battle Interface:**
- Turn order visibility (show upcoming turns)
- Table-view character stats during combat (SF2/3 style)
- Better feedback on damage calculations, hit rates
- Attack/ability range previews

**Exploration & Map:**
- SF2's auto-search function for treasures
- Full map traversability with backtracking
- Secret discoveries and hidden areas
- Map markers/notes for player reference

**Character Progression:**
- XP carryover between level-ups (don't waste experience)
- Promotion at level 20 standardized (SF2 approach)
- Consistent stat gains OR clear communication about randomness
- Alternative promotion paths for variety

**General:**
- Updated, polished translations
- Save anywhere / quicksave options
- Battle speed options
- Skip/fast-forward for repeated animations

### Mechanics Fans Want Preserved

**Sacred Cows - Don't Touch:**
1. **Promotion System** - The transformation moment is core to SF identity
2. **Large Character Roster** - 12 active + reserves, personalization
3. **Exploration Between Battles** - Towns, world map, non-combat content
4. **Forgiving Death System** - Revival at church (permadeath optional only)
5. **Character-Based Units** - Named, personality-driven force members
6. **Accessibility** - Low barrier to entry, can be learned by playing

**Preserve But Refine:**
1. **Simple Combat** - Keep straightforward, don't add complexity for its own sake
2. **Terrain for Mobility** - Not for combat bonuses (unlike FE/TO)
3. **Equipment System** - Weapon durability is NOT part of SF identity
4. **No Weapon Triangle** - Simpler than FE's rock-paper-scissors
5. **Story-Driven Recruitment** - Characters join through narrative, not mercenary hiring (with exceptions)

### Mechanics Fans Want Changed/Added

**AI Improvements (Critical):**
- "The biggest request across all data sources"
- Enemies should use items, spells tactically
- Better positioning and targeting decisions
- Status effect usage by enemies
- Don't skip finishing blows or retreat randomly

**Balance Adjustments:**
- Address class distribution (too many knights)
- Late-joiner stat scaling (don't make them useless)
- Egress exploit mitigation (or make it a toggleable "easy mode")
- More viable build diversity

**Tactical Depth (For Optional Complexity):**
- SF3's friendship system praised as middle-ground
- Status effects more meaningful (but keep accessible)
- Terrain bonuses (optional, toggle-able)
- Support conversations/relationships (FE influence)

**New Content Ideas:**
- Roguelike/endless mode for replay value
- Alternative promotion trees
- Character customization options
- New Game+ features with difficulty scaling
- Recruitable secret characters (more than originals)

### Story/Character Elements Fans Value

**Character Development:**
- Every character having a recruitment moment
- Optional/secret characters as rewards for exploration
- Backstories and personalities (even if not plot-critical)
- Character-specific interactions and dialogue

**World-Building:**
- Exploration of sci-fi/fantasy blend (especially in SF1's Metapha)
- Town NPCs with stories that evolve
- Environmental storytelling
- Lore that rewards attentive players

**Narrative Structure:**
- SF2's open world model preferred over SF1's linear chapters
- Freedom to backtrack and revisit
- Optional content that enriches but isn't mandatory
- Balance between serious drama (SF3) and lighter charm (SF1/2)

### Modding/Customization Desires

**Direct Quotes from Wishlist Discussions:**
- "Modifiable and expandable" systems
- "Open source code and documentation"
- "Freedom to add and develop without being held back by rom modding limitations"
- Toggle options for display styles, difficulty, features

**What This Means:**
- Fans want to CREATE, not just consume
- Frustrated by ROM hacking limitations
- Desire for accessible tools, not just code access
- Asset creation (sprites, maps, characters) is appealing
- Community wants to extend content indefinitely

**Platform Implications:**
- Built-in editor tools would be revolutionary
- Character creator with class system
- Battle map designer
- Item/equipment creator
- Dialogue and story scripting
- Asset import/export capabilities

### Multiplayer & Modern Features

**Existing Multiplayer Experiments:**
- SF2 has hidden co-op mode (accessed via cheat code)
- Fans use it for "Competitive Co-Op" playthroughs via Steam Network Play
- Fightcade 2 online multiplayer (one player controls Force, other controls enemies)
- Battle Royale VS mode concepts discussed: pick 12 characters, symmetrical maps

**Fan Reactions to Multiplayer:**
- Shining Soul II's co-op praised as "AMAZING"
- Vs. mode concepts generate excitement
- Co-op playthroughs popular on streams
- Desire for both cooperative AND competitive modes

**Other Modern Features Discussed:**
- Online leaderboards for challenge modes
- Replay/spectator features
- Mod sharing/browsing in-game
- Cross-platform play
- Achievement systems tied to character unlocks

**Caution**: Fans want these ADDED, not replacing single-player focus. SF is story-driven first.

**Why FE Hacking Thrives:**
- "Most accessible platform, and therefore, the most popular"
- Built on GBA games with extensive documentation
- Permadeath creates dramatic stakes (fan appeal)
- Strong official support keeps franchise alive
- "Community is smaller [than Pokemon] but still highly talented, especially in plot writing"

**SF vs. FE Comparison Insights:**
- FE is "pure strategy focus" while SF is "strategy + RPG exploration blend"
- FE has permadeath tension; SF has accessibility and experimentation
- "Shining Force was the first to really succeed outside of Japan" (historical impact)
- "Without Shining Force, great games like Vandal Hearts, Final Fantasy Tactics and Disgaea probably still would have been made, but they may not have made it to North America as quickly"

**What SF Can Learn:**
- Centralized modding hub (like FEUniverse) builds community
- Accessibility of tools matters more than engine sophistication
- Community-created content extends franchise life indefinitely
- Plot writing talent exists - need to enable it

## 6. Strategic Insights & Recommendations

### What Fans REALLY Want (Beyond Stated Preferences)

**The Data Reveals:**

1. **They Say**: "I want SF4 from SEGA"
   **They Mean**: "I want a modern game that captures what made SF special, from anyone willing to do it right"
   **Evidence**: Fan remake projects, enthusiasm for spiritual successors, active modding scene

2. **They Say**: "Don't change anything from the originals"
   **They Mean**: "Don't change the FEELING and core identity, but please fix the broken parts"
   **Evidence**: Shining Force Alternate praised for AI fixes; GBA remake praised for QoL, criticized for overpowered additions

3. **They Say**: "I love the simplicity"
   **They Mean**: "I love the accessibility, but I'd enjoy optional depth"
   **Evidence**: SF3's friendship system praised; fans self-impose permadeath challenges; interest in roguelike modes

4. **They Say**: "Graphics don't matter"
   **They Mean**: "Graphical FIDELITY doesn't matter, but art STYLE and POLISH absolutely do"
   **Evidence**: GBA remake graphics controversy wasn't about quality, but style; "glorious, high bit pixel art" desired

5. **They Say**: "We just want to play Shining Force again"
   **They Mean**: "We want to CREATE in the Shining Force universe"
   **Evidence**: Active modding despite limitations; fan remake attempts; desire for "open source" and "expandable" tools

### Critical Success Factors for The Sparkling Farce

**Must-Have (Non-Negotiable):**
1. **Preserve SF's Core Identity**: Exploration + tactics blend, character-driven army building, accessible-but-deep
2. **Excellent AI**: This is the #1 complaint about originals - can't repeat mistake
3. **Large, Diverse Character Rosters**: 12+ controllable units, distinct personalities, recruitment moments
4. **Character Promotion System**: Visual transformation, meaningful power spike, player choice of timing
5. **Town/World Exploration**: Can't be just battles - the between-battle content is essential
6. **Forgiving Learning Curve**: Accessible to newcomers, deaths not catastrophic
7. **Mod-Friendly Architecture**: This is already in design - reinforce that fans WILL use it

**Should-Have (Competitive Advantages):**
1. **Powerful, Accessible Modding Tools**: Editor for characters, classes, items, maps, battles
2. **AI That Learns/Adapts**: Go beyond fixing broken AI - make it genuinely challenging
3. **Customization Options**: Toggles for difficulty, display style, optional features
4. **Alternative Progression Paths**: Multiple promotion options, build variety
5. **Endless/Roguelike Mode**: Replayability beyond story campaigns
6. **Quality-of-Life Features**: Modern inventory, turn order display, fast-forward, save anywhere
7. **Community Hub Integration**: Mod sharing, browsing, rating within platform

**Could-Have (Differentiation):**
1. **Co-op & VS Multiplayer**: Both cooperative campaigns and competitive battles
2. **Character Relationship System**: SF3's friendship or support conversations
3. **Dynamic Campaign Events**: Branching paths, player choices affecting story
4. **Advanced Map Editor**: Let users create full campaigns
5. **Asset Import Pipeline**: Custom sprites, tilesets, music
6. **Scripting System**: For custom triggers, events, mechanics
7. **Cross-Platform Mod Sharing**: Steam Workshop integration, web portal

### Positioning Strategy

**The Pitch to SF Fandom:**
"The Sparkling Farce is what we've been waiting for - not a game, but a platform that lets US create the Shining Force experiences we've imagined. It's the tools and freedom we've wanted, without ROM hacking limitations, with modern quality-of-life, and built by people who actually understand why we love this series."

**Key Messaging:**
- "By SF fans, for SF fans" (community-driven)
- "The game is just a mod" (everything is moddable)
- "Create your own Shining Force" (empowerment)
- "Finally, the tools you deserve" (addresses frustrations)

**Differentiation from Competitors:**
- vs. Fire Emblem: "We have the exploration and RPG elements FE lacks"
- vs. FFT/TO: "We have the accessibility and character-focus, not job system complexity"
- vs. Indie Spiritual Successors: "We're not just inspired by SF - we're enabling YOU to create SF"
- vs. ROM Hacks: "No technical limitations, modern tools, unlimited possibilities"

### Risks to Mitigate

**Identified Concerns from Fan Data:**

1. **"Feels Like a 12-Year-Old Made It"**
   - Risk: Poor balance, overpowered additions, fanfic-quality writing
   - Mitigation: Professional polish on base game, curated featured mods, clear quality standards

2. **"Lost the Magic of the Original"**
   - Risk: Modern features that change the fundamental feel
   - Mitigation: Respect SF's design philosophy, make modern features toggleable, "vanilla" mode option

3. **"Another Abandoned Fan Project"**
   - Risk: Perception that it won't be completed or supported
   - Mitigation: Phased releases, transparent roadmap, active community communication

4. **"Gacha/Monetization Nightmare"**
   - Risk: Fans are hyper-sensitive after mobile game disappointment
   - Mitigation: Be 100% transparent about business model upfront, NO randomized monetization

5. **"Too Complex for Newcomers"**
   - Risk: Modding tools intimidate casual players
   - Mitigation: Excellent base game as example, tutorials, templates, "play without modding" option

6. **"Fragmenting the Community"**
   - Risk: Too many mods, hard to find quality content
   - Mitigation: Curation system, ratings, featured mods, compatibility checking

### The Opportunity Window

**Why Now:**
1. **Franchise Dormancy**: SEGA isn't competing - field is open
2. **Nostalgia Cycle**: SF1 (1992) is 32+ years old - prime nostalgia age
3. **Indie Tactical RPG Renaissance**: Genre is hot (Triangle Strategy, Tactics Ogre Reborn, etc.)
4. **Modding Culture Maturity**: Audiences understand and value mod support
5. **Tool Accessibility**: Godot and modern engines enable solo/small team development
6. **Community Hunger**: Active modding scene despite ROM limitations proves demand

**The Window Won't Last Forever:**
- SEGA could revive franchise (unlikely but possible)
- Another spiritual successor could nail the formula first
- Nostalgia audience ages out without new generation engagement
- Genre trends could shift away from tactical RPGs

## 7. Tactical Recommendations for Development

### Phase Priorities Informed by Fan Intelligence

**What to Build First (Validation):**
1. Battle system with GOOD AI - this will make or break credibility
2. Character creation/customization - proves modding vision
3. One complete, polished example battle - shows quality bar
4. Basic editor for characters/classes - demonstrates accessibility

**What Can Wait:**
1. Full campaign/story - fans understand this is a platform
2. Advanced multiplayer - nice-to-have, not essential for validation
3. Complex relationship systems - SF1/2 didn't have this
4. Extensive asset library - community can contribute over time

### Community Engagement Strategy

**Where to Engage:**
1. **Shining Force Central Forums** - Largest, most dedicated community
2. **r/ShiningForce Subreddit** - Active discussions, Reddit visibility
3. **SFMods.com** - Modding community, natural allies
4. **SF Discord** - "Most active currently" per fans

**How to Engage:**
1. **Transparency**: Share development progress, explain design decisions
2. **Solicitation**: Ask for feedback on specific features/mechanics
3. **Recognition**: Credit community members who contribute ideas
4. **Involvement**: Beta testing, asset contributions, mod showcases

**What to Avoid:**
1. Overpromising features/timeline
2. Dismissing "purist" concerns as outdated
3. Comparing unfavorably to Fire Emblem (different strengths)
4. Radio silence - regular updates matter

### Feature Development Validation

**Test These Assumptions with Community:**
1. "Do you want alternative promotion paths, or is single promotion per class sacred?"
2. "Should terrain affect combat stats, or just movement?"
3. "How do you feel about optional permadeath mode?"
4. "What QoL features are essential vs. nice-to-have?"
5. "Would you use an in-game mod browser/installer?"

**Listen For:**
- When fans agree (rare - indicates core value)
- When fans split predictably (indicates need for toggle/option)
- When fans surprise you (indicates blind spot in research)
- When fans get excited (indicates differentiating feature)
